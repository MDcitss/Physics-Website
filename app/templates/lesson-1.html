Lesson 1

The theory of special relativity was developed by Albert Einstein in 1905. It is based on the assumption that the speed of light The speed of light is constant at 299,792,458 m/s. is observed to be the same no matter the relative motion between the observer and the light source.

The theory of special relativity holds within and between inertial frames of reference An inertial frame of reference is a ‘place’ such as on Earth or inside a rocket ship in which all of Newton’s laws are valid. Inside an inertial frame of reference an object with no forces acting on it will travel at a constant velocity in a straight line. . We will only consider objects that have constant relative speeds — no acceleration is involved.

The assumption that the speed of light is constant in all reference frames implies that other quantities, that might ordinarily appear to be constant, are actually relative between observers within different reference frames.

To preserve a constant speed of light between observers moving at a relative speed, space-time Space-time refers to the 4-dimensional expanse that consists of up/down, left/right, in/out and also the one-dimensional flow of time from past towards future. It is the combination of space and time into one ‘place’ that objects can exist within. must appear different to each of them. In the following lesson we will use examples to present this.
