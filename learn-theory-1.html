The theory of special relativity was developed by Albert Einstein in 1905. It is based on the assumption that the speed of light is observed to be the same no matter the relative motion between the observer and the light source.

The theory of special relativity holds within and between inertial frames of reference. We will only consider objects that have constant relative speeds — no acceleration is involved.

The assumption that the speed of light is constant in all reference frames implies that other quantities, that might ordinarily appear to be constant, are actually relative between observers within different inertial frames of reference.

To preserve a constant speed of light between observers moving at a relative speed, space-time must appear different between them. In the following lesson we will use examples to present this.
